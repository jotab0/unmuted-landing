i. Brand Essence

Every voice deserves
to be heard

"Translation isn't replacing words. It's carrying a soul from one language to another."
— Unmuted Philosophy

Mission

To break down language barriers without breaking what makes each person unique. We believe every creator deserves to connect with the world without losing who they are.

Vision

A world where language is never a limit for creative voices. Where a creator in Buenos Aires resonates in Tokyo, Seoul, and São Paulo — and still sounds like themselves.

Values

Authenticity above all — We preserve, never replace.
Humanity — Technology in service of the soul.
Radical quality — "Good enough" isn't enough.
Cultural intelligence — Translating is adapting, not substituting.

Positioning

Unmuted sits between free automated dubbing (but robotic) and expensive enterprise solutions. We are the translation service that prioritizes personality — premium quality, accessible pricing, obsessed with maintaining your essence.

Maintain a minimum space equal to the height of the "U" on all sides. No element should invade this margin of respect.

Backgrounds

Only on clean, neutral backgrounds from the approved palette. Never on images, patterns, or visually noisy backgrounds.

v. Voice & Tone

How we speak

Unmuted speaks like someone who deeply understands both technology and people. We're not a tech company pretending to understand creators — we're people who love human voices and use technology to protect them.

Our tone is warm, close, cultured without being pretentious. We speak with the quiet confidence of those who know what they do, and the humility of those who respect each person's voice more than their own.

Warm, not cold

We speak as people, not machines. Every communication conveys that there are human beings behind it, that we care.

Do

"Your Spanish audience will feel your energy, your humor, your essence."

Don't

"Our processing pipeline maintains the vocal parameters of the input."

Close, not distant

We understand the creator world — the algorithm, the metrics, the pressure to grow. We speak their language.

Do

"Stop losing international watch time to dubbing that sounds like a robot."

Don't

"Optimize your multilingual content distribution pipeline."

Honest, not salesy

AI isn't magic. We're transparent about what we can and can't do. We promise less, deliver more.

Do

"AI does 90% — our human editors perfect the 10% that matters."

Don't

"100% perfect translation with cutting-edge AI."

Cultured, not pretentious

We like beautiful, precise language, but never use it to intimidate or distance. Elegance can also be accessible.

Do

"Every voice has a rhythm, a color, a truth. We carry it to another language without losing anything."

Don't

"Our localization service leverages intercultural synergies."

vi. Visual Language

Imagery & atmosphere

Our visual imagery breathes humanity. Warmth over coldness. Natural textures over plastic surfaces. Real people over generic illustrations.

Photography

Real creators in authentic environments. Natural, warm light. Genuine diversity, not forced. Sincere expressions — laughter, concentration, passion. Never stock photos with headsets.

Textures

Paper, linen, light wood, ceramics. Textures that feel tactile and human. Subtle paper grains in backgrounds. Everything that reminds us that behind technology there are real hands and voices.

Composition

Generous space. Room to breathe. Elegant asymmetry inspired by editorial design. Images don't compete with text — they coexist in harmony.

vii. Brand Usage

What we do & what we don't

Consistency builds trust. These rules keep Unmuted elegant and human at every touchpoint.

Do

  • Always write "Unmuted" — one word, capital U
  • Use warm, neutral backgrounds as base: ivory, cream, bone, linen
  • Prioritize Playfair Display in italic for editorial moments
  • Leave generous space — air is part of the design
  • Use real photography with natural light and human warmth
  • Write in a warm, close, and honest tone
  • Combine editorial elegance with accessible language
  • Use accents (blush, terracotta, sage) sparingly and purposefully

Don't

  • Never use dark backgrounds as the dominant color — we're not tech bros
  • Never use generic sans-serif fonts (Inter, Roboto, Arial)
  • Never use saturated colors, neon, or aggressive gradients
  • Never use stock photos of robots, circuits, or AI hands
  • Never use corporate jargon: "leverage", "synergy", "best-in-class"
  • Never promise AI perfection — always be honest
  • Never design with excessive density — respect the space
  • Never lose humanity — we are people serving people
